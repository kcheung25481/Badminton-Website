How to Play

Court information

The badminton court layout exists in such a way to allow for both singles and doubles play. This can be seen on the left and right sides of the court, where there is an extra 1.5 feet on either side of the court when doubles play is involved. For the back, the outer most line is used when a rally has started.

For services the birdie must land cross-court, and past the service line 6.5 feet in front of the net, as well as behind the second line from the back.

Proper Grip

There are 4 main ways to grip the racquet, Forehand, Bevel, Thumb, And Panhandle. The forehand grip is used for most shots when playing a rally. When in doubt, use this grip as it is the most optimal grip for lifts, smashes, drops, and many more.

The bevel grip is mainly used for backhand shots where the birdie is behind you. Shift your grip slightly so the raqcuet is angled on a offangle, which allows for powerful backhand shots.

The thumb grip is the other grip used for backhand shots, this time when the birdie is close to your body or in front of you. This allows for powerful shots as you use your thumb to generate power on the raquet to push the birdie.

The last grip is the Pandhandle or Hammer grip. This grip is used in drives, as this generates power fast and perpendicular to your body, which is what you want when playing a fast drive rally.

Types of Play

There are 5 different variations of badminton. Men's Singles, Women's Singles, Men's Doubles, Women's Doubles, and Mixed. As the names suggests, singles is played with 1 person, doubles is played with 2 (both of the same gender), and mixed is played with 1 male and 1 female. Mixed games have the same ruleset as doubles games.
